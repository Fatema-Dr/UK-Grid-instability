# Project Dissertation Structure and Topics

Based on `Lesson 7 - Remaining Chapters.pptx`, the following structure and topics must be included in the final dissertation report.

## 1. Title Page (Front Cover)

## 2. Abstract

## 3. Acknowledgement (Optional)

## 4. Table of Contents / Figures / Tables

## 5. Chapter 1: Introduction

## 6. Chapter 2: Literature Review

## 7. Chapter 3: Methodology

## 8. Chapter 4: Results / Findings
*   **Purpose:** Present findings only (facts). **No interpretation** in this chapter.
*   **Typical Structure:**
    *   **4.1 Introduction**
    *   **4.2 Implementation Outcomes**
        *   Wireframe designs
        *   E-R diagrams
        *   Screenshots of the application
    *   **4.3 Research Outcomes**
        *   Survey summary tables
        *   Survey diagrams (graphics, charts)
    *   **4.4 Conclusion**

## 9. Chapter 5: Analysis / Discussion
*   **Purpose:** The most important part; engage the reader in critical thinking and interpret the data.
*   **Key Topics:**
    *   **Implementation Reflection:** What worked? What didn't? How well did you meet the objectives?
    *   **Research Interpretation:** Formulate a deeper understanding of the problem. How do you interpret the data?
    *   **Implications:** Discuss the underlying meaning, importance of the study, and contributions to the field (filling gaps).
    *   **New Gaps:** Did findings reveal new gaps in literature?

## 10. Chapter 6: Conclusion
*   Includes sections like **Future research and recommendations** (e.g., Section 6.5).

## 11. References
*   Must use the **Harvard System**.

## 12. Appendix
*   **Warning:** Do **not** use as a dumping ground. Supervisors may not read it. Only include additional material (e.g., sample survey questionnaires, full source code snippets, extra screenshots).
*   **Typical Structure:**
    *   **Appendix A:** Sample survey questionnaire
    *   **Appendix B:** Source code (e.g., PHP/JavaScript)
    *   **Appendix C:** Additional screenshots
